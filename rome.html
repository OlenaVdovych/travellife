COLOSSEUM

Perhaps the best-preserved of the monuments of ancient Rome, this huge marble structure was built to hold more than 50,000 spectators to witness bloody contests of might and the slaughter of wild beasts. Amazing place, well worth a visit, stunning views, must do a tour to hear the information, worth doing an early visit, skip the line tour, and if possible the Forum afterwards which is also pretty awe inspiring!

PANTHEON

The pleasing Pantheon (a former Roman temple, and now a church) is indeed an amazing architectural work of marvel and wonder, for which its perfect and everlasting dome has been emulated by many architects, even after many centuries later. It was built around 125AD, for Hadrian. It is one of the best preserved, of ancient roman buildings. It is indeed stunning that almost two thousand years after it was built, the Pantheon's dome is still the world's largest unreinforced concrete dome.

PIAZZA NAVONA

The most outstanding square of the Baroque period in Rome features Bernini's Fountain of Rivers at its center as it faces Borromini's church, Sant' Agnese in Agone. The square is a fine example of Baroque Architecture, the central fountain is a masterpiece of Bernini "The Four rivers Fountain", the atmosphere is absolutely amazing.

TREVI FOUNTAIN

Certainly the most famous and photographed fountain in Rome, legend has it that whoever throws a coin into the pond will return to Rome. Lovely place to visit this fountain is huge and you have to visit to truely appreciate this monument. You have to keep with tradition and throw coins over you shoulder back to fountain and make a wish.

ROMAN FORUM

This area is old heart of Rome.Today it's only some ruins, but 2000 years ago it was a heart of the most powerful empire. You can expect clear description signs for each ruined building. There is official ticket office nearby crossroads of Via Dei Fori Imperiali and Via Cavour where it's possible to buy combined ticket for Colosseum, Roman Forum and Palatine hill.
